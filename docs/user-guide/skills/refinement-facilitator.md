# refinement-facilitator (Scrum Master)

Makes refinement sessions shorter and sharper: prep cards and a timeboxed agenda before, faithful capture of the team's estimates and decisions after. The team's time goes to deciding, not discovering — and the skill never estimates on the team's behalf.

## When to use

- **Prep mode:** the day before a refinement session
- **Capture mode:** right after the session, while notes are fresh

## Before you start

- Prep: backlog access; DoR results if the critic ran; who's attending
- Capture: your (or the scribe's) session notes

## What happens

**Prep:**

1. It pulls candidates — top-of-backlog unrefined items, `dor-needs-work` stories whose blockers got answered, PO-flagged urgencies — and confirms the list with you.
2. Each candidate gets a prep card: what it is, AC state, open questions (and which got answered since last time), dependencies, and *specifically what it needs from the team* (estimate? split decision? technical answer?).
3. The agenda is timeboxed and questions-first (decision items before estimate-only items), with a parking lot for rabbit holes.
4. **You approve**, then it posts the agenda where the team expects it.

**Capture:**

5. From your notes it extracts per story: estimate, agreed splits, requested AC changes, decisions, new questions with owners.
6. **You confirm the capture**, then it writes story points and a decision comment per story (attributed to the session date, not to individuals). Splits and AC edits go to the PO as proposals — it doesn't rewrite stories.

## What gets written

Agenda post (prep); story point values + decision comments (capture).

## Good to know

- If more than a third of candidates aren't DoR-ready, it will tell you the session will be discovery, not refinement — run the [critic](definition-of-ready-critic.md) first.
- Prep cards state facts and questions, never scope recommendations — that's the PO's call in the room.
