# Tabletop Shakedown: T4 — SM Ceremony Sweep on AP Blue's Active Sprint

**Date:** 2026-07-11 | **Mode:** live read-only | **Run log:** `.ai-sdlc/runs/2026-07-11-tabletop-shakedown-t4-sm-ceremony-sweep.md`
**Hypothesis:** Explore a live sprint's full ceremony cycle with AP Blue's active sprint (KDP) to discover which SM-skill data needs — velocity history, planning commitment record, flags, activity signals, sprint goal — are unavailable or dirty in the real instance.
**Scenario source:** Sprint "AP Blue S89 7.6-7.17" (board 3020, 26 issues), closed sprints AP Blue S85–S88, KDP backlog top-100 by rank, KDP flagged/blocked sweeps, Confluence NDTW/PMO/KDP-space ceremony pages. Discovery JQL: `project = KDP AND sprint in openSprints()`, `sprint in closedSprints() AND resolved >= -12w`, `statusCategory = "To Do" ORDER BY rank`, `cf[11266] is not EMPTY`, `status = Blocked`.

## Headline

The hypothesis held, with one inversion. All eight SM skills can *start* on this sprint today — none is hard-blocked — but seven of eight run degraded because the data they lean on hardest is missing or polluted: **no planning commitment record exists anywhere**, **81% of AP Blue's recently completed items carry no story points** (velocity is uncomputable as points), **the Flagged field is simultaneously unused by the team and polluted with 50+ never-cleared flags from 2022–2024**, and **`openSprints()` returns four "active" sprints including one open since November 2023**. The inversion: the datum teams most often lack — a sprint goal — is AP Blue's strength, set on every sprint S85–S89 in outcome language. The skills' *checks* would surface real problems on day one; their *numbers* would be dishonest without the reconstruction and fallback modes proposed below.

## Part 1 — Content findings (defects in the scenario's Jira/Confluence)

Per the tabletop's blameless constraint, findings describe work items and record-keeping, never people.

### Blocking-grade

1. **No commitment record exists for any AP Blue sprint.** No Confluence planning/commitment page, no baseline snapshot, nothing. S89's committed scope on 2026-07-06 is unrecoverable from current field state — the sprint field shows *current* membership only, and per-issue changelogs are the only archaeology left. *(sprint-report-generator — its rule "If the commitment record doesn't exist (planning wasn't captured), say the baseline is reconstructed and how" fires immediately; sprint-planning-facilitator's `Commitment Record` template section has never been filled by anyone.)*
2. **Velocity history is effectively absent.** Of 54 AP Blue items resolved in closed sprints S85–S88 (200-issue sample, most-recent-resolved), 44 (81%) carry no story points. Completed points per sprint: S85 = 2, S86 = 11, S87 = **0** (11 items closed, all unpointed), S88 = 12 — against 44 points sitting in S89 today. A "min/median/max of recent sprints" computed from this would be numerically valid and factually meaningless. *(sprint-planning-facilitator, workflow step 1.)*
3. **`openSprints()` is polluted with zombie sprint containers.** Project-scoped discovery returns 84 issues across four "active" sprints: AP Blue S89 and AP Red S89 (real), plus "AP Modernization Q2S4" (end date 2026-06-09 — 32 days overdue, goal empty, 29 issues) and "Release 1 - Sprint 20" (end date **2023-11-24** — active for ~32 months). Any skill that queries "the active sprint" without board/team scoping ingests three teams and two ghosts. *(surfaced walking daily-standup-digest's input "The active sprint (Jira board/sprint)"; affects every sprint-scoped skill.)*
4. **The Flagged field is dead and dirty at the same time.** Zero flagged items in the active AP Blue or AP Red sprints — the team does not use the field. Project-wide, 50+ items have Flagged set, essentially all Done/Closed relics from 2022–2024 whose flags were never cleared (only one flagged item is in-flight, on another team). Blocked-status rot compounds it: 44 items sit in status Blocked, many untouched 1–3 years; KDP-40596 (the .NET 10 blocked item from the previous tabletop) is still Blocked and now belongs to **no sprint**, so sprint-scoped scans no longer see it at all. *(impediment-radar — its "Explicitly flagged" and "Recurrence" signals would read pure noise.)*
5. **Six in-flight sprint items have no assignee** — four In Test in DEV (KDP-40558, KDP-40574, KDP-40609, KDP-40611) and two Deployed to QA (KDP-39650, KDP-40758). Work in test states with no owner is the same "who is testing?" defect T1 found on the .NET 10 epic, now confirmed in the active sprint. *(daily-standup-digest / impediment-radar.)*

### Significant

6. **Silent stalls exist and nothing marks them.** Four of the twelve in-progress-category items show no Jira activity for 2+ working days as of 2026-07-11: KDP-40397 (quiet since sprint day 1), KDP-40611, KDP-40686, KDP-40728. KDP-40397 is the poster child: In Progress across its **fifth consecutive sprint** (S85→S89), 0 points, empty AC field, one comment since mid-May — unflagged, unblocked, invisible to any signal except activity analysis. *(daily-standup-digest "Stalled" rule; impediment-radar "Silent stalls… the dangerous kind" and "Patterns".)*
7. **The sprint goal is recycled, and the misses are unrecorded.** S87 and S88 goals are verbatim identical ("Complete all outstanding Bulk Upload 2.0 work items and plan the production deployment."); S89's is a light edit of the same. The goal was therefore missed at least twice with no outcome statement anywhere. The honest headline the review facilitator must compute — "did we do what we said?" — is answerable from data and the answer is "missed, twice, silently." *(sprint-review-demo-facilitator, step 1.)*
8. **Scope churn is heavy and reason-free.** 7 of 26 S89 items (27%) were created after sprint start (five already Done or in PO validation — fast-lane unplanned work); 11 of 26 (42%) carried over from S88. No scope-change reason is recorded on any of them. Data oddity: KDP-40776, created 2026-07-07, carries the *closed* S88 sprint in its sprint field — sprint-field history can actively mislead reconstruction. *(sprint-report-generator "Scope changes always show when they happened"; retro-facilitator evidence pack.)*
9. **Half the sprint has no acceptance criteria — along a clean type boundary.** 13 of 26 items have empty AC: all five Bugs, all six Story Bugs, the long-running testing item, and one aged bug. Every Story and Tech Managed - Deployable item has substantial AC (some ~10K chars). Likewise all six unpointed items are Story Bugs. Two unwritten team conventions — "bugs don't get AC" and "bugs don't get points" — are consistently applied and recorded nowhere. *(sprint-planning-facilitator rule "Unestimated or non-ready stories can enter the candidate list only flagged as such"; DoR-adjacent.)*
10. **The project backlog is decayed and rank is not a signal.** Top-100 `To Do` by rank: 54% untouched >90 days; median created-age 421 days; 56 items over a year old. The top 20 ranked items include QA sub-tasks created 1,000–1,571 days ago belonging to defunct teams — team-field values include "x_AC Core (DO NOT USE)" and personal-named teams of departed staff. 15 of the top 20 story-level items are unestimated. Rank order represents no team's actual next work. *(backlog-hygiene-auditor — every category fires except the two below.)*
11. **Zero pipeline vocabulary exists in the wild.** No `dor-ready`, `dor-needs-work`, `retro-action`, or `hygiene-archived` label appears anywhere in KDP. Every skill check keyed on library labels (refinement candidate selection, hygiene aging check, retro action tracking) is inert on first contact. *(refinement-facilitator, backlog-hygiene-auditor, retro-facilitator.)*
12. **AP Blue has no retro record.** AP Red publishes retro pages in NDTW that record initial vs. final commitment and completed points (the exact behavior our skills produce); QAA does too. For AP Blue: no retro page, no sprint report, no planning page — only a DRAFT working agreement (Jun 3). The retro-facilitator's mandatory leading section has no source at all. *(retro-facilitator, step 2.)*
13. **A cautionary precedent already lives in the instance.** A "DAAP Sprint Goal Generator" bot publishes AI-generated sprint-goal pages to the KDP Confluence space — including four abandoned pages titled "test"/"trash" from a single day of experimentation, still current four months later. Ungated AI ceremony artifacts without cleanup discipline are already visibly littering the instance. *(system-relevant observation; hygiene-auditor has no Confluence-side check.)*

### What the content gets right

- **Sprint goals, five sprints running.** Every AP Blue sprint S85–S89 has a goal, phrased as an outcome a stakeholder can read. Most real-world boards have none; this is the single datum our skills usually have to beg for, present in an unbroken streak.
- **Team field discipline: 26/26** S89 items carry Team = AP Blue — team-scoped queries work, which is what rescues discovery from the `openSprints()` pollution.
- **Cadence discipline.** Consecutive two-week sprints, each closed within ~1 business day of its end date (S88 closed 7/6 for a 7/3 end — over a weekend). The ceremony rhythm is real.
- **Where AC and points exist, they're rich** — stories carry up to 10K chars of structured AC. The gaps follow a consistent type convention, i.e., this is an unrecorded policy, not chaos.
- **The org already demonstrates the target behavior unevenly:** AP Red's retro pages contain commitment-vs-delivery prose; the PMO is actively standardizing ceremonies (Sprint Planning and Sprint Retrospective standard pages, and a joint Sprint Review pilot agenda naming AP Blue, edited the day before this tabletop). The skills have a customer and a Confluence home waiting.

**Elaboration worth keeping:**

- **T4-S1/T4-S2 are the load-bearing pair.** Every quantitative promise the SM skills make (velocity range, done-vs-committed pulse, carryover rate, commitment-vs-delivery table) sits on two records this instance doesn't keep. Both have honest fallbacks — throughput and declared reconstruction — and both fix themselves going forward the first time planning-facilitator's record mode runs. The report generator's own rule already anticipated this; what's missing is the shared *procedure* the rule implies.
- **T4-S4 validates the radar's multi-signal design while gutting one signal.** The previous tabletop praised the radar for scanning multiple signals because KDP-40596 was flag-invisible. This tabletop shows the flag signal isn't just sometimes-missing — in this instance it's *anti-signal* (100% of hits are relics). The skill text treats the field as trustworthy; it should treat it as a claim to verify against status and activity.
- **T4-S5's first-run problem generalizes.** The hygiene template already handles it ("Previous audit: {date-or-first}"); the retro template doesn't; the radar's "Previous snapshot: {date-or-first}" does. First contact with a real team is the *common* case for a new library — every skill with a "compare to last time" step needs a defined first-run posture.
- **T4-S13 context for the librarian:** the DAAP bot's trash pages are what our approval gates exist to prevent. Worth citing when anyone asks why the gates are non-negotiable.

## Not exercised

- **GitHub-side signals** (standup-digest step 1 "PRs opened/merged… CI failures"; radar "Aging reviews") — no team→repo mapping exists and this tabletop had no repo access. Named as T4-S8 rather than silently skipped.
- **Capture/record/after modes** of refinement-facilitator (session capture), sprint-planning-facilitator (commitment record), and retro-facilitator (action capture) — they require a live ceremony and human notes; walked on paper against their templates only.
- **All approval gates and every posting/write step** of all eight skills — read-only tabletop; no SM was consulted, no digest posted, no escalation drafted for sending.
- **Sprint-wide unanswered-question scanning** (digest signal) — spot-checked one issue's comments only; a full sweep needs comment pagination across 26 issues and was out of scope.
- **Demo-environment verification** (review facilitator step 4) — no environment access; every scripted item would carry "NEEDS VERIFICATION".

## Verdict

The scenario proved that the SM skills' failure mode on real data is not refusal but **quiet dishonesty**: every skill would produce a plausible-looking artifact today, and four of the numbers in those artifacts (velocity range, committed baseline, flag-based impediment age, done-vs-committed pulse) would be fabrications unless the skills are taught to say "not recorded", "reconstructed", or "not computable" — exactly the postures T4-S1/S2/S4 add. The instance is better prepared than expected in the places that need judgment (goals, team scoping, cadence, an org that already runs retros) and worse in the places that need bookkeeping (points, commitment records, flags, labels, rank). One skill — backlog-hygiene-auditor — could run at full strength today and would drown its approvers, which is its own finding. Highest-value next scenario: a **live first-run pair** — planning-facilitator record mode at the S89→S90 boundary to create the org's first commitment record, then standup-digest daily for one sprint with a declared Jira-only banner — turning T4-S2 and T4-S8 from proposals into tested behavior.
