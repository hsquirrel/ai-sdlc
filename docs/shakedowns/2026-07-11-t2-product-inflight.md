# Tabletop Shakedown: Product-Driven In-Flight Epic (KDP-40761)

**Date:** 2026-07-11 | **Mode:** live read-only | **Run log:** `.ai-sdlc/runs/2026-07-11-tabletop-shakedown-t2-product-inflight.md`
**Hypothesis:** Explore product-driven, in-flight work with KDP-40761 (Legacy to DAO Modernization — Name Change, DOB Change, SSN/TIN Update) + all children to discover whether, even on the pipeline's home turf (product work with rich house-style AC), adoption gaps and content drift appear — and whether the real house AC stress-break the definition-of-ready-critic and test-plan-generator.
**Scenario source:** KDP-40761 + children KDP-40800, 40807–40811 (6 stories; KDP-40801–40806 do not belong to this epic — 40801 is an unrelated Task, the rest don't exist); siblings KDP-40759, KDP-40797; initiative TR-8; Confluence pages 9929326593, 9922510869 et al.

## Headline

The hypothesis held, but not where expected. The house AC survive the DoR critic's AC items almost untouched — they are the best AC either tabletop has seen (correct field, exact house style, atomic, titled). The failures all moved **up one level**: every story has an empty description (no role, no story framing, no NFR, no open questions), the child set defines the same screen **twice** (composite KDP-40800 vs. its five-way re-slice KDP-40807–40811, all six Open simultaneously), the epic's number-one dependency ("must be delivered first") has no Jira issue to link, and all three modernization epics are orphans while their obvious parent initiative (TR-8) sits In Progress. The single most important system lesson: the DoR checklist is per-story and catches *contradiction* but is blind to *duplication* — the exact set-level defect this epic exhibits.

## Part 1 — Content findings (defects in the scenario's Jira/Confluence)

Each finding names the skill and rule that surfaced it. These are real defects, fixable today.

### Blocking-grade

1. **The same screen is specified twice, in parallel, with no supersession marker.** KDP-40800 ("DAO Work Item Creation Screen & Functionality", created 07-09, 6 ACs) is a composite story whose AC body was then re-sliced into KDP-40807–40811 (created 07-10) — with **verbatim** overlaps: KDP-40810's AC#1–2 are word-for-word KDP-40800's AC#1–2; KDP-40811's six ACs re-state 40800's AC#4; 40808 restates 40800 AC#3's field specs; 40809 restates AC#5; 40807 restates the AC#3 banner plus AC#6. All six are Open with no links between them. Whichever set gets refined first silently orphans the other, and the first divergent edit creates a real contradiction with two plausible sources of truth. *(Surfaced while applying definition-of-ready-critic item 12 set-scan; hygiene-auditor "likely duplicates" would miss it — see finding 13 and proposal T2-S1.)*
2. **All six stories have completely empty descriptions.** No "As a {role}, I want…" anywhere; the AC say "a user" while the epic's own Background says the creator persona is the advisor (Home Office reviews, and HO-initiated creation belongs to the infrastructure epic). No NFR block, no open questions, no links — the entire story is the AC field. Every story is **Not ready** on DoR item 1 [blocking] alone. *(definition-of-ready-critic, item 1.)*
3. **The epic's hardest dependency is un-linkable because its target doesn't exist in Jira.** KDP-40761's description: "**General maintenance infrastructure epic** — Maintenance Selection Framework, Create Work Item screen, evidence upload, PCF screen, Signing Packet Service, MaintenanceSigned API, and Direct IDM Update API **must be delivered first**." No such epic exists in Jira (searched KDP epics for Maintenance/DAO Modernization) — it exists only as a Confluence draft decomposition ("DAO Maintenance Migration Epic and Stories", page 9929326593, 10 fully-formed stories, never written to Jira). Nothing can be linked, watched, or flagged; the dependency lives as prose. The ARB gate is similar: ARB-656 sits at status "ARB Not Ready" but is linked as **Related**, not "is blocked by," so link-based dependency tooling ignores it. *(DoR critic item 10; impediment-radar dependency-rot check has no object to watch.)*
4. **The epic's Out-of-Scope list contradicts itself in place.** Bullet: "NFS Failed-Edit auto-create path (SubType 120)" — with the nested note directly under it: "Needs to be included if we are doing NFS API updates. So this should NOT be out of scope." The epic simultaneously declares the item out of scope and not out of scope; there is no recorded resolution, owner, or question. *(DoR critic item 12 contradiction scan; product-brief-builder's scope rule would have forced the call in the interview.)*

### Significant

5. **Decomposition covers ~1 of 6 in-scope areas for 1 of 3 subtypes, with no marker of the remaining plan.** All six stories are Client-Name-Change creation-screen UI. Nothing for DOB or SSN/TIN subtypes, forms/e-signature (incl. the POA e-sign block), submission, routing, IGO/NIGO, the hold model, or status tracking. The epic is 6 days old so incompleteness is expected — but no spike, placeholder, or open question records what's still to slice, and KDP-40800's title promises "…& Functionality" while no AC in the set covers submitting anything. *(backlog-decomposer step 4 traceability check; DoR critic item 2.)*
6. **The success metric is a literal placeholder.** "X% reduced NIGO rates?" — while the Background contains an unusually good baseline (39% NIGO on 1,422 Name/DOB items, 45% on 125 SSN/TIN items in 2025). Baseline without target — the inverse of the usual failure. The trailing ARB questionnaire prompts ("Eligible for STP?", "Do we need to build UI screens…?") sit unanswered in the description, and one In-Scope bullet is cut off mid-sentence: "Migration from Legacy - All ". *(product-brief-builder rule: every outcome needs baseline → target; open-questions table exists for exactly these prompts.)*
7. **All three modernization epics are orphans while their parent initiative exists.** KDP-40761, KDP-40759, KDP-40797 have no parent; TR-8 "Advisor Platform Modernization" (In Progress, assignee Connie Ross) is the initiative KDP-40761's own Background names ("As part of the Advisor Platform Modernization effort…"). Cross-project parenting is supported (kdp-schema notes epics can parent to TR initiatives). *(DoR critic item 9; hygiene-auditor orphan check.)*
8. **KDP-40807 AC#4 is circular and the upload contract is undefined.** "**When** the user selects a file from the local file explorer **Then** the user can select a file to attach" — the When and the Then are the same event; no derivable expected outcome. Nowhere in the set: permitted file types, file-size limits, multi-file behavior, or failure paths — for a workflow whose entire purpose is document-based review. *(DoR critic item 5 [blocking] for 40807; test-plan-generator would return it as a top finding.)*
9. **Zero negative or edge AC across the whole set.** No required-field enforcement (Middle Name is "(Optional)", implying the rest are required — never stated), no behavior at/over the 24-character name limit (blocked? truncated? error?), no character-set rule for legal names (hyphens, apostrophes, unicode), no client-with-zero-accounts case, no upload failure path. The 24-char limit itself deserves a PO question: real legal names exceed it, in an epic whose stated purpose is legally accurate name records. *(DoR critic item 6; exploratory-charter-generator risk picture.)*
10. **The access model is unspecified where it matters most.** KDP-40800 AC#4 / KDP-40811 AC#2: "the dropdown contains the full name of all Client records **the user has access to**" — access rules undefined in story, epic, and linked pages. A tester must invent the permission fixture; for a PII-heavy flow this is also the security-relevant AC. *(DoR critic item 5; test-plan-generator.)*
11. **No design assets linked on six user-facing UI stories** — no mockup, Lucid, or Design Story, and no "no design needed" statement. The epic links a "02 Front-End Migration: UI Components" analysis page, but no story links anything. *(DoR critic item 11.)*
12. **Field hygiene noise.** Children carry Team = "Product Backlog" (a bucket, not a team — the field's semantics are being used as a stage marker); the epic carries irrelevant infra form-defaults (Browser=Chrome, Server=CHSQL08QAC01, OS=Windows 2019 Standard) that mean nothing on an epic; sibling KDP-40797 has "(WIP)" in its title and an empty description. Zero comments exist anywhere in the family — the NFS-120 scope dispute (finding 4) looks like a discussion pasted into the description instead. *(hygiene-auditor; jira-confluence-writer conventions.)*
13. **Duplication is invisible to every existing detector.** The writer's duplicate check keys on *same summary under same parent* (all six summaries differ); the hygiene auditor pairs *similar summaries*; the DoR critic scans for *contradictions*. Verbatim-duplicated AC under different summaries — this epic's actual defect — slips through all three. *(Cross-skill; drives proposal T2-S1.)*

### What the content gets right

- **The AC are the house style done properly**: right field (customfield_14705), `AC#N: title` bold, bold Given/When/Then, one AC per block with rules between — and 40807–40811 are textbook-atomic (one behavior each, titled so a failure names itself). The kdp-schema's house style example is literally drawn from this team's pattern, and here it's honored perfectly.
- **The Background field is a genuine product brief**: business objective, business context with quantified evidence (NIGO counts with numerators and denominators), a four-point why-now including compliance/PII rationale, problem, and outcome. This is the second epic in two tabletops whose Background field is ~90% of a brief — it's a house convention, not an accident.
- **The Requirements field is a real functional spec**: complete two-layer status model (Base + Sub-Status) with per-status advisor-facing copy, per-subtype state flows, e-sign vs. wet-sign paths, POA restriction, Omni-Channel skill configuration. A test designer could trace expected results from it directly.
- **Traceability is far better than T1**: 8 Confluence remote links (gap analysis, validation rules, migration overview, UI components, CRM-sync determination), a Related link to the sibling epic, and ARB items created per epic.
- Explicit Out-of-Scope with reasons; dependencies enumerated per owning team (Advisor Platform vs. Salesforce team); honest inline notes ("DOB sub type does not currently exist…").

**Elaboration on T2-S1 (the big one).** This tabletop's central defect — two slicing generations coexisting — is the *in-flight* signature of product work: decomposition is iterative, and re-slicing without supersession is how a healthy backlog grows a contradiction. Three skills each have a near-miss detector (contradiction, summary-similarity, summary-equality), and the actual failure mode threads between all three. AC-body comparison within an epic is cheap and precise. The critic's report should present the pair with a proposed survivor and a proposed action for the loser (close as superseded, or link "is replaced by"), gated on the PO as usual.

**Elaboration on T2-S3.** T1 said the Background field "is 80% of a brief already" (T1-S1); T2 confirms it as a convention, with structure stable across authors (Mark Douglas's 40761, Hema Punater's 40759 use the same section skeleton, both with unanswered ARB prompts). The pipeline should stop treating this as an anomaly to work around and start treating it as the house brief format to read and write.

## Appendix A — Skill-by-skill walk (pipeline order)

### product-brief-builder

No brief page exists, but one is reconstructible at high fidelity from the epic itself: Background (cf14757) supplies Problem (with quantified evidence), Users/Roles (advisors, HO Account Services), Outcome, Why-Now; the description supplies Scope In/Out/Dependencies. Against the template, the gaps the interview would have forced: **metric target** (rule: "baseline → target shape" — baseline 39%/45% NIGO is known, target is "X%?"), **stakeholder/approver table** (TR-8's Connie Ross, reporter Mark Douglas — nowhere recorded), **open questions classified blocking/non-blocking** (the unanswered ARB prompts and the NFS-120 dispute are exactly this table's content), and resolution of the self-contradicting Out entry (rule: "Scope must contain explicit 'Out' entries" — present, but one entry argues with itself). Verdict: the skill's questions fit this scenario's humans perfectly; only its *entrance* (interview for new work) and *output location* (Confluence page) mismatch — see T2-S3.

### backlog-decomposer

The house decomposition is vertical in intent (per-subtype, per-workflow-area) but the executed slices are widget-horizontal: one screen cut into a story per field group, plus the uncut composite still open beside them. Under the skill's written rules the six stories *pass* ("small enough to finish within a sprint" — no floor exists; see T2-S2), while the set fails the skill's purpose (no story is independently demoable as user value; nothing submits). Traceability markers ("which brief item each epic serves") are absent but reconstructible from titles. The unknowns the skill would have converted to spikes/open questions instead live as prose notes: "DOB sub type does not currently exist", the access model, upload constraints.

### jira-confluence-writer

What it would have done differently, per its written rules: parented the epic (step 2 forces the Initiative question; TR-8 exists), populated story descriptions with framing + "Open Questions" heading (step 5), left Team empty rather than "Product Backlog" (rule: leave team fields to refinement), labeled everything for auditability, and typed dependency links. What it could **not** have prevented: the semantic duplication (its dedup key is same-summary-under-same-parent — see finding 13/T2-S1) and the prose dependency on a nonexistent epic (create-only scope; see T2-S4). Credit: the content already follows its AC-placement and house-style rules exactly — evidence the house style the schema documents is real practice, not aspiration.

### exploratory-charter-generator — what the risk profile would produce (paper mode; entrance not met)

1. **Explore** the supervised-field hold model **with** concurrent work items (two name changes in flight; name + SSN change on one client) **to discover** state-machine breaks in commit/discard — *high; the hold model is this epic's compliance core*.
2. **Explore** name inputs **with** real-world legal names (hyphens, apostrophes, unicode, >24 chars) **to discover** truncation/rejection of legally accurate names — *high; requirements-shaped, route to PO now*.
3. **Explore** the NIGO → correct → resubmit loop **with** interruptions and repeat NIGOs **to discover** drift between Base Status and Sub-Status — *high; two-layer status model is new*.
4. **Explore** document upload **with** boundary abuse (wrong types, huge files, password-protected PDFs, double-submits) **to discover** intake robustness the AC never constrain — *medium*.
5. **Explore** Legacy-originated vs. DAO-originated client records **with** dirty legacy data **to discover** parity failures in the "all clients" promise — *medium*.
6. **Explore** client-record visibility **with** advisor/HO role matrix **to discover** PII permission leaks in the undefined access model — *high*.

## Verdict

On its home turf the pipeline's AC-level machinery mostly *passes* real house content — the DoR critic's AC items (4, 5) and the test-plan generator's derivation rules confirm these are good AC with specific, nameable exceptions (40807 AC#4, the access-model ACs, the 24-char limit). The stress moved to levels the per-story checklist covers thinly or not at all: story identity (all descriptions empty), set-level duplication (two slicing generations, invisible to every current detector), and dependency reification (the most important dependency has no issue to link). The hypothesis — adoption gaps and content drift appear even on product work with rich AC — is confirmed, with the refinement that here "content drift" means *the backlog disagreeing with itself*, not the backlog disagreeing with the epic. Highest-value next scenario: T3 (historical replay) to test whether these same set-level defects show up as the root causes of a completed effort's real failures, or T6 (developer role) to finally exercise the unexercised half of the library.
