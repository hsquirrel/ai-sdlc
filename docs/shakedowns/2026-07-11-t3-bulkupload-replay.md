# Tabletop Shakedown: Bulk Upload 2.0 Historical Replay (KDP-38143)

**Date:** 2026-07-11 | **Mode:** historical replay | **Run log:** `.ai-sdlc/runs/2026-07-11-tabletop-shakedown-t3-bulkupload-replay.md`
**Hypothesis:** Explore a completed/mature effort with the Bulk Upload 2.0 family's full recorded history to discover which real, historically-recorded failures the pipeline artifacts (brief, DoR gate, decomposition traceability, test plans, readiness labels) would have prevented — and which not.
**Scenario source:** Initiative KDP-38143 + 18 children (deep-dive epics KDP-38089, KDP-38132, KDP-38179; churn epics KDP-39790, KDP-39924); 172 Bug/Story Bug issues matching "Bulk Upload"; ~50 Confluence pages across NAO, SRE/APS, NDTW, QA2, KRN, JS including "ARB Review: Bulk Upload 2.0" (NDTW 9242673208). Strictly read-only; no changelog API access — history reconstructed from resolution dates, automation comments, and human comments.

## Headline

The hypothesis held, but not where expected. This team's *story-level* content is decent — stories carry real AC, spikes precede solution work, an ARB process exists — so the pipeline's flagship story gates (DoR critic, house AC style) would have changed little. The failures history actually records are almost all **epic-lifecycle and boundary failures**: a scope dispute recorded in a comment on day one and never resolved in any artifact; four epics closed as Duplicate; epics closed "Complete" with open children and open linked bugs; remaining work silently reparented into carryover-bucket epics; a governance review permanently "Proposed/Pending" while the system shipped; and a 37% noise rate across 172 filed bugs. The brief, the decomposer's traceability, the hygiene auditor, and the bug-report-writer would have prevented or sharply reduced most of these — but the pipeline has **no close-time gate, no mid-flight change control, and no governance-artifact tracking**, and those three gaps account for the most expensive failures on the record.

## Part 1 — Content findings (defects in the scenario's Jira/Confluence)

### Blocking-grade

1. **The founding scope question was never answered in an artifact.** Connie Ross's day-one comment on KDP-38089 (incremental fixes vs. new solution) is the exact question a brief's Scope/Out/Later section exists to settle; it was settled by drift into "1.0"/"2.0" epic naming instead, and the initiative carries no scope statement or success metric to this day. *(product-brief-builder — rules on scope with explicit non-goals and blocking open questions; the initiative fails its own template's sections 3, 5, 7.)*
2. **Epics were closed "Complete" while their work was open.** KDP-38179 resolved 2025-10-29 with child KDP-38183 open until 2025-12-05; KDP-38089 resolved Complete with linked action-item bug KDP-38074 Open and four child bugs quietly reparented to KDP-39924. The Complete resolution's own definition ("no outstanding issues") is contradicted by the data both times. *(Surfaced by DoR-critic-style contradiction scanning applied at epic level; no current skill owns this check — see T3-S1.)*
3. **A closed-Duplicate epic still owns active work.** KDP-39790's closure comment claims all children were moved to KDP-40164; KDP-38344 remains parented to the closed epic and is Deployed to QA as of 2026-07-09. Any epic-scoped query, report, or board misses in-flight work. *(backlog-hygiene-auditor — zombie-epic check, inverted case.)*
4. **A Blocked epic with an invisible blocker.** KDP-39924 has status Blocked, Flagged unset, zero comments, no blocked-by link; the blocker (ARB-498 "ARB Not Ready") is discoverable only by opening a "relates to" link. Flag- and link-based impediment queries all miss it. *(impediment-radar — validates its multi-signal design; only the status signal fires.)*
5. **Governance closed the loop on paper only.** "ARB Review: Bulk Upload 2.0" has been "Proposed / Pending" since Oct 2025, with unfilled security/compliance checklists, an empty risks table, and an inline unanswered question about row-data encryption — while the system it reviews shipped to L2 validation (Confluence "Bulk Upload 2.0 Team Familiarization & Validation", Jul 2026). Its author's account is deactivated; nobody owns it. *(tech-design-drafter — violates its rules: open questions unlisted, doc not linked from any driving Jira issue, decision never dated/owned.)*

### Significant

6. **Four epics closed as Duplicate** (KDP-38127, KDP-39049, KDP-39709, KDP-39790) — one created and duplicate-closed within 48 hours, one duplicated by a *newer* epic that won. Epic-level duplication means decomposition was happening ad hoc, in meetings, with no registry to check against. *(backlog-decomposer — its draft-and-trace workflow is the missing registry.)*
7. **37% bug noise.** 64 of 172 bugs closed without a fix; Not An Issue (13) and Duplicate (10) classes are directly preventable — expectations not anchored to any AC, and no duplicate search before filing (e.g., the Government-ID requirement filed four times across four issue types; KDP-32025 is literally titled "CLONE -"). *(bug-report-writer — steps 2 and 4 target exactly these.)*
8. **A Story Bug parented to an Initiative.** KDP-40288 (hierarchy level −1) hangs directly off KDP-38143 (level 2), where no story-level tooling will ever find it. *(jira-confluence-writer's kdp-schema / bug-report-writer's kdp-bug-types — "parent = the story under test".)*
9. **Scope moves leave no ledger.** "Remaining work has been moved to 2.0" (comment, 2026-03-30) is the only record of what moved from KDP-38089 to where; KDP-39924 inherited its description by verbatim copy-paste; KDP-39790's children "all" moved except the one that didn't. *(sprint-report-generator tracks scope changes within a sprint; nothing tracks them across epics — see T3-S5.)*
10. **Epic-as-meeting-minutes.** KDP-39790's description is an unedited meeting summary (options discussed, attendee tasks, "Chloe can take on the work"), with a reference link labeled "cleaning rules for 2.0" pointing to a page titled "Compensation Statement". *(backlog-decomposer / product-brief-builder — raw material masquerading as an artifact.)*
11. **Bugs used as epic children instead of Story Bugs.** 19 of KDP-38089's 40 children are type Bug; only 10 of 172 bugs system-wide use Story Bug. In-story defect rates per story are unrecoverable from the data. *(bug-report-writer classification rules.)*
12. **The 2026-01-12 "Declined" mass sweep** closed ~10 aged bugs with no recorded rationale — the audit-with-reasons the hygiene auditor performs on cadence, done instead as an untraceable bulk write-off. *(backlog-hygiene-auditor — "items kept/closed get their reason recorded".)*
13. **Requirements live in Confluence, invisible from Jira.** "Bulk upload 2.0 High Level Deliverables Requirement", "User Requirements & Decision Summary", validation-rules pages, and the ARB review are unlinked from the epics; a stale "DRAFT -" validations copy in NDTW coexists with the current NAO pages. Most of this documentation was written Feb–Jun 2026 — after the code. *(every skill that reads "linked context"; reconfirms T1-S7.)*
14. **A cross-project epic (QAA-964) and a near-permanent "PO Validated" story bug (KDP-38354, 10 months)** sit under/around the initiative with no stated reason or owner follow-up. *(hygiene auditor orphan/stale checks; impediment-radar silent-stall check.)*

### What the content gets right

- **Stories have real AC** — nearly every story child we sampled has a populated Acceptance Criteria field (up to 12.5KB on the help-text stories). The DoR critic would have passed most stories on items 4–5. This team's defect was never "no AC".
- **Spikes before solutions** (KDP-38063, KDP-38078 precede the macro-removal work) — exactly the decomposer's prescribed shape for unanswerable AC.
- **An ARB process exists** with per-epic ARB tickets — the failure is follow-through, not absence.
- **Delivery comments on closed epics** (KDP-38132's closing summary is a model), duplicate closures carry cross-links, and the initiative description is a genuine problem statement, not a solution statement.
- **The 2.0 Confluence estate is rich and organized** (hubs, validation rules, SOPs, op-readiness) — the content exists; only the linkage and timing failed.

## Part 2 — System findings (proposals to change the library)

| ID | Skill / convention | What misfired (cite the rule) | Proposed change | Status |
|----|--------------------|-------------------------------|-----------------|--------|
| T3-S1 | backlog-hygiene-auditor (or new close-gate check) | Zombie-epic check covers "open epics whose children are all done or all stale" — the **inverse** of what happened here (epics *closed* with open children/linked work: KDP-38179, KDP-38089, KDP-39790) | Add a **closure-integrity check**: resolved epics with open children, open linked action-items, or recently-active descendants are a standard finding; recommend an epic-closeout checklist (children resolved or explicitly re-homed with a ledger entry, linked bugs dispositioned, delivery comment present) | proposed |
| T3-S2 | backlog-decomposer + jira-confluence-writer | Decomposer governs initial decomposition ("every epic names the brief item it serves"); writer is create-once. Nothing governs **mid-flight epic creation** — all 4 duplicate epics and both carryover buckets were created ad hoc, months after any initial decomposition would have been approved | Make the decomposition doc a **living registry**: a rule that new epics under a pipeline-managed initiative are drafted against the registry (which brief item? does an epic already serve it?) and the registry updated; hygiene auditor flags epics under such initiatives lacking a brief-item marker | proposed |
| T3-S3 | bug-report-writer | Steps 2/4 anchor expected-to-AC and search duplicates, but nothing calibrates against the **area's closure history** — this area's 37% noise rate (13 Not An Issue, 12 Cannot Reproduce, 10 Duplicate) was visible in Jira the whole time | Add a rule: before filing, scan recent closed bugs in the same area for non-fix resolutions; a high Not-An-Issue/Cannot-Reproduce rate triggers stricter evidence demands (env/build captured, behavior-question routing to PO per the existing rule). Also map the local "Training" resolution → propose help-text/docs work instead of a defect | proposed |
| T3-S4 | impediment-radar | Dependency-rot check watches "'is blocked by' links to other teams' issues"; KDP-39924's real blocker is a **'relates to' ARB ticket in a gating status** ("ARB Not Ready"), and the epic is Blocked with no comment/flag/link at all | Two additions: (a) **Blocked-without-recorded-blocker** as an explicit finding type (status Blocked + no flag, no blocking link, no explaining comment); (b) treat linked governance/review tickets (ARB etc.) in not-ready/bypassed states as dependencies subject to rot detection | proposed |
| T3-S5 | convention (decomposition doc + sprint-report-generator) | Sprint report shows scope changes *within a sprint*; no artifact records **scope moved between epics/versions** — "moved to 2.0" exists only as a comment, and the 39790→40164 move silently lost a child | Add a **moved-scope ledger** section to the decomposition/registry doc (what moved, from→to, when, why, by whom); hygiene auditor verifies reparent events against it | proposed |
| T3-S6 | definition-of-ready-critic / dor-checklist | The DoR is story-scoped (its only epic touch is item 9, "the story has a parent Epic"); this effort's stories were largely fine — the readiness failures were **epic-level** (no scope statement, no metrics, no linked design/ARB with status, meeting-minutes descriptions) | Add an **epic-readiness checklist** (own file or dor-checklist section): epic names the brief/initiative item it serves; scope stated (not meeting minutes); success measure present; design/governance artifact linked *with its review status*; closure criteria stated. KDP's own Epic type description already demands AC on epics — the check has native cover | proposed |

Elaboration on the two biggest:

**T3-S1 (closure integrity) is the highest-value single change.** Three of the five blocking-grade findings are the same event — an epic resolved while its work wasn't — and the failure is cheap to detect (resolved parent + open/active descendants is one JQL join). The auditor's current zombie rule proves the category belongs to it; it just points the wrong way.

**T3-S6 reframes what "readiness" means for this work shape.** The hypothesis assumed the DoR gate would be the star of the replay; the data says otherwise — the stories were ready, the *epics* never were. A pipeline whose only readiness gate is story-level would have graded this effort green at exactly the altitude where it was failing.

## Not exercised

- **Developer skills** (implementation-planner, copilot-handoff-packager, code-review-critic, pr-hygiene) — no repo access; the KestraBulkUpload ADO repo is referenced from the ARB page but unreachable here. The KDP-38096 commit-coupling failure suggests a future T6 should include ADO ticket-branch policies.
- **Readiness labels as such** — `dor-ready`/`dor-needs-work` are pipeline outputs; the effort predates them, so the replay evaluated the gate, not the labels. No label-shaped evidence existed to compare against.
- **SM ceremony skills in depth** (sprint-planning, standup-digest, refinement, demo, retro) — no sprint/board data was pulled; T4 owns that hypothesis. sprint-report-generator and retro-facilitator were walked only against the scope-churn evidence.
- **release-notes-generator, ac-playwright-scaffolder, exploratory-charter-generator** — no release-note fields or test-session artifacts in the gathered set.
- **Jira changelog** — the MCP toolset exposes no changelog expansion on search; status-duration claims rely on resolutiondate/updated/comments (automation comments proved a serviceable substitute).

## Verdict

The replay confirms the hypothesis with a twist that matters: the pipeline's front door (brief → decompose → write → DoR) would have prevented the framing and duplication failures, and the tester/SM checks would have cut the bug noise and surfaced the stalls — but this effort's costliest recorded failures happened at **transitions the pipeline doesn't guard**: epics closing over open work, scope moving without a ledger, and a governance review dying in "Pending" while the code shipped. A gated system that only gates *entry* would have graded this effort green at every point where history graded it red. T3-S1 (closure integrity) and T3-S6 (epic-level readiness) are the cheapest fixes with the largest evidence base; T3-S2/S5 (living registry + moved-scope ledger) address the churn mechanics. Next-highest-value scenario: **T9 (cancelled/pivoted epic)** — this replay brushed against scope death four times (four Duplicate epics) and the library still has no answer for it; T6 (developer/repo access) would let the KDP-38096 "no" row be tested rather than conceded.
