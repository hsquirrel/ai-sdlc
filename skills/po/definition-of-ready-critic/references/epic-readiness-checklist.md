# Epic Readiness Checklist

Readiness is two-altitude: the story DoR (`dor-checklist.md`) guards stories, this checklist guards epics. An effort whose stories are green but whose epics fail here is failing where it's expensive (T3 replay: Bulk Upload 2.0's stories were ready; its epics never were). Items marked **[blocking]** force "Not ready."

1. **[blocking] Traceability** — the epic names the brief/initiative item it serves (registry marker per the decomposition registry) or states why it stands alone. An epic that duplicates an existing epic's brief item is a finding, not a second registry entry.
2. **[blocking] Scope is authored, not transcribed** — the epic's description states what it delivers and what it does not; raw meeting minutes, attendee task lists, or a pasted copy of another epic's description all fail.
3. **[blocking] Epic AC present** — objective completion criteria in the AC field (KDP's own Epic type definition demands this). "All children done" is not an AC; what observable outcome makes this epic *complete*?
4. **Success measure** — how the epic's outcome will be judged (metric, demoable result, or explicit inherit-from-initiative), stated on the epic.
5. **[blocking] Governance/design artifacts linked with status** — if the work needs an ARB review or design doc, it is linked from the epic *with its current review status visible*. A review sitting "Proposed/Pending" while children are in flight is a blocking finding with an owner question, not background noise.
6. **Closure criteria** — what must be true to resolve this epic (feeds the closeout checklist at the other end of its life).
7. **Open questions owned** — epic-level open questions (scope disputes, stakeholder objections in comments) are recorded with owners; a scope question raised in a comment and unanswered for more than a sprint is blocking.
